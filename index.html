Cast care guide

The experience should educate as it reassures. These are the practical reminders most users need every day.

Daily habits

Keep the cast dry, avoid putting objects inside it, and raise the injured limb to reduce swelling. Moving fingers or toes gently can also help circulation when it’s safe to do so.

Keep it dryUse protection in the shower and avoid soaking the cast.
Elevate oftenSwelling usually feels better when the limb is raised above the heart.
Don’t insert objectsScratchers, pencils, and powders can irritate skin and damage the cast.

Get help sooner if

A cast should not cause escalating pain, numbness, blue or pale digits, or reduced movement. Wet, cracked, loose, or foul-smelling casts also need attention.

Severe pain or tightnessEspecially if elevation doesn’t help.
Numbness or tinglingThis can signal too much pressure or circulation issues.
Color changeCold, pale, blue, or purple fingers and toes are not normal.
